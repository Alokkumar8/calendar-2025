Public Holidays in the United States in Calendar 2025

As we look ahead to the year 2025, understanding the key public holidays in the calendar 2025 is important for planning. The US celebrates a range of holidays throughout the year, and being familiar with the 2025 calendar can help you prepare for these events. Below is a detailed list of all the public holidays for the year 2025, as well as key dates on the calendar 2025 that you should know about:

1. New Year's Day - January 1, 2025

New Year's Day marks the beginning of the new year on the 2025 calendar. This day, celebrated by millions, signals a fresh start. As we welcome in 2025, many Americans reflect on their resolutions and plan their year ahead using the calendar 2025 as a guide.

2. Martin Luther King Jr. Day - January 20, 2025

Observed annually on the third Monday of January, Martin Luther King Jr. Day in 2025 will be an important date in your calendar 2025. It’s a day of remembrance and reflection on Dr. King's legacy of peace and justice. Many will mark this holiday by checking their calendar 2025 for community service opportunities.

3. Presidents' Day - February 17, 2025

Presidents' Day in 2025, observed on the third Monday in February, is another key day on the calendar 2025. This day is dedicated to celebrating US presidents, especially George Washington, whose birthday falls around this time. The 2025 calendar will be marked by parades, sales, and ceremonies.

4. Memorial Day - May 26, 2025

Memorial Day in 2025, observed on the last Monday of May, is a day to honor fallen soldiers. As you look at your calendar 2025, this holiday stands as a reminder of the sacrifices made by military personnel in defense of the country. Many Americans will use this calendar 2025 date to spend time with family and remember the heroes of the nation.

5. Independence Day - July 4, 2025

Independence Day, or the Fourth of July, is one of the most anticipated holidays in the calendar 2025. This day is marked by fireworks, parades, and national pride as Americans celebrate the birth of their nation. You will certainly want to mark this important day on your 2025 calendar.

6. Labor Day - September 1, 2025

Labor Day, the first Monday in September, celebrates the contributions of workers and the labor movement in the USA. It is an important date on the 2025 calendar, often marking the unofficial end of summer. As you check your calendar 2025, keep in mind that many businesses will be closed in observance of this national holiday.

7. Columbus Day - October 13, 2025

Columbus Day, which falls on the second Monday in October, will be observed on October 13 in 2025. This date on the calendar 2025 commemorates the arrival of Christopher Columbus to the Americas in 1492, though it remains a controversial holiday for many. As you plan your 2025 calendar, keep in mind the varying observances across the country.

8. Veterans Day - November 11, 2025

Veterans Day in 2025 is observed on November 11, honoring military veterans. This day on the calendar 2025 is marked by parades and remembrance ceremonies across the country. It’s a day to express gratitude to all veterans who have served in the US Armed Forces.

9. Thanksgiving Day - November 27, 2025

Thanksgiving is celebrated on the fourth Thursday of November in 2025, which falls on November 27. This holiday is a time for family gatherings and feasts, often marked by turkey, stuffing, and pumpkin pie. Many people rely on their calendar 2025 to plan long weekend trips or to prepare for the large meal.

10. Christmas Day - December 25, 2025

Christmas Day in 2025, observed on December 25, is one of the most widely celebrated holidays in the United States. It is marked by festive gatherings, gift-giving, and religious observances. This important date on the 2025 calendar is enjoyed by millions of families around the nation.

Additional Holidays:

11. Groundhog Day - February 2, 2025

On Groundhog Day, celebrated on February 2, Americans watch to see if the groundhog sees its shadow. This quirky tradition is a part of the 2025 calendar, and while it doesn't dictate the weather, it has become an amusing tradition for many families and communities.

12. Easter Sunday - April 20, 2025

Easter Sunday is a major religious holiday for Christians, and in 2025, it falls on April 20. This date in the calendar 2025 marks the resurrection of Jesus Christ and is widely observed with church services, egg hunts, and family celebrations.

13. Halloween - October 31, 2025

Halloween, celebrated on October 31, is a fun and spooky holiday for kids and adults alike. Whether you are dressing up or handing out candy, it is an important date on the calendar 2025 for many Americans who look forward to costumes, decorations, and haunted house tours.

14. New Year's Eve - December 31, 2025

New Year's Eve, celebrated on December 31, marks the end of the year and the beginning of the next. It is a significant moment on the 2025 calendar, with countdowns, fireworks, and parties filling the night as people prepare to welcome the new year.

Each of these holidays is an important part of the American calendar 2025, offering a mix of reflection, celebration, and tradition. Whether you're marking religious observances, honoring historical figures, or enjoying family time, these days shape the fabric of life in the United States. Be sure to check your calendar 2025 to ensure you don’t miss out on any important events!
