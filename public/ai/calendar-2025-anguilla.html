Public Holidays in Anguilla in Calendar 2025

As we look ahead to the year 2025, understanding the key public holidays in the calendar 2025 is important for planning. Anguilla observes a variety of public holidays throughout the year, and being aware of these dates can help you organize vacations and special events. Below is a detailed list of all the public holidays in Anguilla for the year 2025.

1. New Year's Day - January 1, 2025

New Year's Day marks the start of the new year on the 2025 calendar. It is a time for celebrations and gatherings, as people in Anguilla welcome the new year with joy, parties, and fireworks.

2. James Ronald Webster Day - March 2, 2025

This day honors James Ronald Webster, the "Father of the Nation," for his role in leading Anguilla to greater autonomy. The holiday is marked with ceremonies, speeches, and celebrations of national pride.

3. Good Friday - April 18, 2025

Good Friday is a solemn day in Anguilla, observed by Christians to commemorate the crucifixion of Jesus Christ. Many attend church services, and it is a day of reflection and prayer.

4. Easter Monday - April 21, 2025

Easter Monday follows Easter Sunday and is celebrated with family picnics, beach outings, and community events. It is a day of relaxation and enjoyment for residents and visitors alike.

5. Labour Day - May 5, 2025

Labour Day in Anguilla recognizes the contributions of workers to the nation. The day is often celebrated with parades, cultural events, and community activities.

6. Anguilla Day - May 30, 2025

Anguilla Day commemorates the beginning of the Anguilla Revolution in 1967. It is a national holiday celebrated with parades, boat races, and other festive activities that reflect the island's rich culture and history.

7. Whit Monday - June 9, 2025

Whit Monday, observed the day after Pentecost, is a public holiday in Anguilla. It is often a day for community gatherings and religious observances, celebrating the descent of the Holy Spirit upon the apostles.

8. August Monday - August 4, 2025

August Monday is a highlight of the Anguilla Summer Festival. It features colorful parades, music, dancing, and boat races. It is one of the most vibrant celebrations on the island.

9. August Thursday - August 7, 2025

August Thursday is another key day during the Anguilla Summer Festival. It is marked by boat races and other cultural events, showcasing the island's maritime heritage and festive spirit.

10. Constitution Day - August 8, 2025

Constitution Day celebrates the adoption of Anguilla's Constitution. The day is marked by ceremonies, community events, and a continued focus on national pride and governance.

11. National Heroes and Heroines Day - December 19, 2025

This holiday recognizes the contributions of individuals who have played significant roles in shaping Anguilla's history and culture. It is a day of remembrance and national pride.

12. Christmas Day - December 25, 2025

Christmas Day celebrates the birth of Jesus Christ and is a joyous occasion in Anguilla. Families gather for festive meals, exchange gifts, and attend church services.

13. Boxing Day - December 26, 2025

Boxing Day is a day for relaxation and spending time with family and friends. Many in Anguilla enjoy outings to the beach or participate in community activities, continuing the festive spirit of Christmas.
