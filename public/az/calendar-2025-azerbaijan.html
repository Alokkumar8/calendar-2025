Public Holidays in Azerbaijan in Calendar 2025

As we look ahead to the year 2025, understanding the key public holidays in the calendar 2025 is important for planning. Azerbaijan observes a variety of public holidays throughout the year, reflecting its rich history, culture, and traditions. Being aware of these dates can help you organize vacations and special events. Below is a detailed list of all the public holidays in Azerbaijan for the year 2025.

1. New Year's Day - January 1, 2025

New Year's Day marks the beginning of the new year and is celebrated with great enthusiasm across Azerbaijan. Families gather for festive meals, and cities are decorated with lights and fireworks, symbolizing hope and joy for the year ahead.

2. International Women’s Day - March 8, 2025

International Women’s Day is a celebration of women’s achievements and contributions to society. It is observed with flowers, gifts, and appreciation for women in families and workplaces across the country.

3. Novruz Bayram (Spring Festival) - March 20-24, 2025

Novruz Bayram, the Azerbaijani New Year and Spring Festival, is one of the most important holidays in the country. It marks the arrival of spring and is celebrated with traditional foods, music, dances, and symbolic activities like jumping over bonfires.

4. Victory Day over Fascism - May 9, 2025

Victory Day commemorates the end of World War II and honors those who fought against fascism. The day is marked with parades, ceremonies, and tributes to veterans and war heroes.

5. Republic Day - May 28, 2025

Republic Day celebrates the establishment of the Azerbaijan Democratic Republic in 1918. It is a significant national holiday marked by patriotic events, flag-raising ceremonies, and public celebrations.

6. National Salvation Day - June 15, 2025

National Salvation Day marks the return of Heydar Aliyev to political power in 1993, which is considered a turning point in Azerbaijan's modern history. The day is observed with events highlighting national unity and progress.

7. Armed Forces Day - June 26, 2025

Armed Forces Day celebrates the establishment of the Azerbaijani Armed Forces in 1918. It is a day to honor military personnel and their contributions to national security, often with military parades and ceremonies.

8. Gurban Bayram (Eid al-Adha) - March 31-April 1, 2025

Gurban Bayram, also known as Eid al-Adha, is an Islamic holiday celebrated by offering prayers and sharing meals with family, friends, and those in need. It commemorates the willingness of Prophet Ibrahim to sacrifice for his faith.

9. Independence Day - October 18, 2025

Independence Day celebrates Azerbaijan’s declaration of independence from the Soviet Union in 1991. The day is marked with national pride, cultural events, and commemorations of the country’s achievements.

10. Constitution Day - November 12, 2025

Constitution Day commemorates the adoption of Azerbaijan’s first post-Soviet constitution in 1995. It highlights the principles of democracy and governance in the country.

11. National Revival Day - November 17, 2025

National Revival Day marks the start of the national independence movement in 1988. It is a day to reflect on Azerbaijan’s journey toward sovereignty and freedom.

12. Day of Solidarity of World Azerbaijanis - December 31, 2025

This day celebrates the unity and solidarity of Azerbaijanis around the world. It is a time to connect with Azerbaijani heritage, culture, and values, bringing people together for shared celebrations.

13. Ramazan Bayram (Eid al-Fitr) - March 29-30, 2025

Ramazan Bayram, also known as Eid al-Fitr, marks the end of Ramadan, the holy month of fasting. It is celebrated with prayers, feasts, and acts of charity, bringing families and communities together.

14. Flag Day - November 9, 2025

Flag Day celebrates the adoption of Azerbaijan’s national flag in 1918. It is a day of patriotic pride, with flag-hoisting ceremonies and educational activities promoting the importance of national identity.

15. Memorial Day - September 27, 2025

Memorial Day honors the memory of those who sacrificed their lives during the Second Karabakh War. It is observed with moments of silence, tributes, and national remembrance.
