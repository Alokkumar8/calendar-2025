Public Holidays in Albania in Calendar 2025

As we look ahead to the year 2025, understanding the key public holidays in the calendar 2025 is important for planning. Albania observes a variety of public holidays throughout the year, and being aware of these dates can help you organize vacations and special events. Below is a detailed list of all the public holidays in Albania for the year 2025.

1. New Year's Day - January 1-2, 2025

New Year's Day is celebrated over two days in Albania, marking the beginning of the new year. Families come together to celebrate with feasts, fireworks, and cultural traditions. It is a time for joy, resolutions, and welcoming the year ahead.

2. Summer Day - March 14, 2025

Summer Day, known as "Dita e Verës," is a popular Albanian holiday that celebrates the arrival of spring. It is traditionally associated with Elbasan, where festivities include parades, concerts, and the baking of ballokume, a traditional cookie.

3. Nevruz Day - March 21, 2025

Nevruz Day is a public holiday celebrated by the Bektashi community in Albania. It signifies the Persian New Year and the arrival of spring. People celebrate with family gatherings, traditional meals, and prayers for prosperity.

4. Catholic Easter - April 20, 2025

Catholic Easter is an important religious holiday in Albania for the Catholic community. It commemorates the resurrection of Jesus Christ and is celebrated with church services, feasts, and family gatherings.

5. Orthodox Easter - April 27, 2025

Orthodox Easter is celebrated by Albania's Orthodox community, marking the resurrection of Jesus Christ. The holiday includes midnight church services, traditional foods like lamb, and the cracking of red-dyed eggs.

6. International Workers' Day - May 1, 2025

International Workers' Day is a public holiday in Albania, dedicated to honoring workers and labor rights. Many people take the day off to relax, spend time with family, or attend peaceful demonstrations.

7. Eid al-Fitr - March 31, 2025

Eid al-Fitr marks the end of Ramadan, the holy month of fasting for Muslims. It is a joyous occasion with prayers, festive meals, and acts of charity. Albanian Muslims celebrate by visiting friends and family and sharing traditional dishes.

8. Independence Day - November 28, 2025

Independence Day, known as "Dita e Pavarësisë," commemorates Albania's declaration of independence from the Ottoman Empire in 1912. It is a day of national pride, celebrated with ceremonies, flag-raising events, and patriotic displays.

9. Liberation Day - November 29, 2025

Liberation Day celebrates Albania's liberation from Nazi occupation during World War II in 1944. It is marked by national celebrations, tributes to the country's heroes, and cultural programs.

10. Christmas Day - December 25, 2025

Christmas Day is celebrated by Albania's Christian communities with religious services, family gatherings, and traditional feasts. Many people also decorate Christmas trees and exchange gifts during this festive season.
