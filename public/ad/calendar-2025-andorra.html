Public Holidays in Andorra in Calendar 2025

As we look ahead to the year 2025, understanding the key public holidays in the calendar 2025 is important for planning. Andorra observes a variety of public holidays throughout the year, and being aware of these dates can help you organize vacations and special events. Below is a detailed list of all the public holidays in Andorra for the year 2025.

1. New Year's Day - January 1, 2025

New Year's Day is celebrated with joy and enthusiasm as it marks the beginning of the new year. Families gather for special meals, and people take part in festivities to welcome the year ahead.

2. Epiphany - January 6, 2025

Epiphany, also known as Three Kings' Day, is a significant holiday in Andorra. It is celebrated with parades and traditional festivities, marking the visit of the Magi to the infant Jesus.

3. Constitution Day - March 14, 2025

Constitution Day commemorates the adoption of Andorra's Constitution in 1993. It is a day of national pride, celebrated with official ceremonies and various events throughout the country.

4. Good Friday - April 18, 2025

Good Friday is a solemn day observed by Christians in Andorra. It commemorates the crucifixion of Jesus Christ with church services and moments of reflection.

5. Easter Monday - April 21, 2025

Easter Monday follows Easter Sunday and is a public holiday in Andorra. It is often celebrated with family gatherings and outings to enjoy the spring season.

6. Labor Day - May 1, 2025

Labor Day is celebrated on May 1st to honor workers' rights and achievements. It is a day off for most people and an opportunity to relax or participate in labor-related events.

7. Whit Monday - June 9, 2025

Whit Monday is observed 50 days after Easter and is a public holiday in Andorra. Many people use this day for relaxation or attending church services.

8. Saint John’s Day - June 24, 2025

Saint John’s Day, or Festa de Sant Joan, is a midsummer celebration in Andorra. Bonfires and traditional dances are central to the festivities, symbolizing the triumph of light over darkness.

9. Assumption of Mary - August 15, 2025

This holiday honors the assumption of the Virgin Mary into heaven. It is marked by religious ceremonies and celebrations in churches across Andorra.

10. Andorra la Vella Festival - August 28, 2025

The Andorra la Vella Festival celebrates the patron saint of the capital city. The festivities include cultural performances, music, and traditional activities that attract locals and tourists alike.

11. All Saints' Day - November 1, 2025

All Saints' Day is a Christian holiday dedicated to honoring all saints, known and unknown. Families visit cemeteries to pay respects to their departed loved ones.

12. Immaculate Conception - December 8, 2025

This holiday celebrates the Immaculate Conception of the Virgin Mary. It is observed with religious services and traditional events in Andorra.

13. Christmas Day - December 25, 2025

Christmas Day is a festive occasion celebrating the birth of Jesus Christ. Families come together to exchange gifts, enjoy festive meals, and attend midnight mass.

14. Saint Stephen's Day - December 26, 2025

Saint Stephen's Day, also known as Boxing Day, is celebrated the day after Christmas. It is a time for relaxation, family gatherings, and charitable acts.

15. Our Lady of Meritxell Day - September 8, 2025

This is Andorra’s National Day, dedicated to Our Lady of Meritxell, the patron saint of the country. The day is celebrated with religious ceremonies and cultural activities across the nation.
